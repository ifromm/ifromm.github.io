Dr. Ingo Frommholz is a senior lecturer in computer science at the Department of Computing Science and Technology of the University of Bedfordshire in Luton, UK. Prior to this he worked as a research associate at the University of Glasgow, the University of Duisburg-Essen and the Fraunhofer Integrated Publications and Information Systems Institute (IPSI), where he contributed to a number of European (FP5, FP6 and FP7) and national German and British projects. He gained further experience as a software developer in various companies in Germany. Ingo received his PhD in 2008 at the University of Duisburg-Essen on the topic of probabilistic logic-based information retrieval models. His current research focuses on probabilistic inference and quantum-based geometrical models for interactive information access. He has a strong expertise in information retrieval, digital libraries and information systems and is the Managing Editor of the International Journal on Digital Libraries.
